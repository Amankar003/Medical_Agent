=========================================
MEDICAL GUIDELINES FOR COMMON AILMENTS
=========================================

1. Management of Viral Fever:
Viral fevers are typically self-limiting and last for 3 to 5 days. Typical symptoms include sudden onset of high temperatures, fatigue, mild cough, body cramps, and sweating. 
Treatment Protocol: 
- Take maximum rest to allow the immune system to recover.
- Keep hydrated by drinking at least 2.5 to 3 liters of fluids daily (water, coconut water, oral rehydration solutions).
- Antipyretics like Paracetamol can be consumed to bring down the body temperature. Avoid antibiotics as they do not work against viruses.
Red Flag: If the fever crosses 103°F (39.5°C) and lasts more than 3 days, or is accompanied by difficulty in breathing, consult emergency services immediately.

2. Management of Acid Peptic Disease (Acidity):
Acid Peptic Disease is characterized by heartburn, stomach pain, feeling of fullness, and nausea, usually triggered by diet or stress.
Treatment Protocol:
- Antacids (containing magnesium hydroxide and aluminum hydroxide) provide rapid symptomatic relief.
- Maintain a gap of at least 2 hours between your last meal and sleep.
- Elevate the head of your bed by 6 inches.
- Avoid spicy foods, caffeine, carbonated drinks, and smoking.

3. First Aid for Minor Cuts and Bleeding:
- Wash hands thoroughly with soap.
- Stop the bleeding by applying gentle, firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
- Clean the wound using cool running water. Do not use hydrogen peroxide or iodine as they can irritate the tissue.
- Apply an antibiotic ointment and cover the wound with a sterile bandage.
Red Flag: If the cut is deep, gaping, or bleeding heavily after 15 minutes of constant pressure, visit a hospital for stitches.

4. Coping with Migraines:
Migraines are severe, throbbing headaches usually localized to one side of the head, and often accompanied by light/sound sensitivity and nausea.
Prevention and Care:
- Retreat to a dark, quiet room at the first sign of a migraine.
- Apply a cold or warm compress to the head or neck.
- Stay adequately hydrated.
- Maintain regular sleep schedules.
